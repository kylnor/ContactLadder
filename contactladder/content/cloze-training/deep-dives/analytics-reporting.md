## MODULE AN2: ACTIVITY ANALYTICS

### Tracking Your Outreach & Engagement

**WHY (Video - 3-4 min)**

"Ninja Selling says relationships drive business. Activity Analytics proves whether you're actually building those relationships or just thinking about it. Here's the deal: 50 outreaches but only 5 responses? Your message isn't landing."

**WHAT (Text + Visual)**

### Activity Metrics Explained

**Outreach = YOU initiated contact:**
- Emails sent (first contact in thread)
- Calls made (outbound)
- Texts sent (first in conversation)
- Notes added

**Reactions = THEY responded:**
- Emails replied to
- Calls returned
- Texts sent back
- Meetings accepted

**Key Ratios:**

| Ratio | What It Measures | Healthy Range | What It Means |
|-------|------------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Outreach:Reaction** | Contacts to get 1 response | 3:1 to 5:1 | Lower = better engagement |
| **Email Open Rate** | % emails opened | 25-40% | Higher = better subject lines/timing |
| **Response Rate** | % outreaches that get responses | 20-35% | Higher = stronger relationships |

### What Cloze Activity Analytics Shows

**Daily/Weekly/Monthly Views:**
- Total outreaches
- Total reactions
- Outreach/reaction ratio
- Trends over time (getting better or worse?)

**By Contact Type:**
- Outreach to Advocates vs. Network
- Response rates by Segment
- Identify which segments need more attention

**By Channel:**
- Email outreach vs. reactions
- Phone outreach vs. reactions
- Text outreach vs. reactions
- Which channel gets best response?

**HOW (Video - 4-5 min)**

*Screen recording showing:*

### Part 1: Access Activity Analytics

1. **Go to Analytics ‚Üí Activity**
2. **Select time period:** This week, this month, this quarter, or custom date range
3. **View metrics:** Outreach count, reaction count, ratio visualization

### Part 2: Interpret Your Activity Data

**Example Report:**
```
January 2025 Activity:
- Outreaches: 145 | Reactions: 38 | Ratio: 3.8:1 ‚úÖ (healthy)
- Email: 95 sent, 22 responses (23%) ‚úÖ
- Phone: 30 calls, 10 callbacks (33%) ‚úÖ
- Text: 20 texts, 6 responses (30%) ‚úÖ
- Trend: +12% outreach vs. last month
```

**Analysis:** Healthy ratios across board. Phone has highest response rate ‚Üí prioritize calls. Outreach increasing ‚Üí good momentum.

**Example Problem:**
```
January 2025 Activity:
- Outreaches: 220 | Reactions: 18 | Ratio: 12.2:1 ‚ö†Ô∏è (poor)
- Email: 200 sent, 12 responses (6%) ‚ö†Ô∏è
- Phone: 15 calls, 4 callbacks (27%) ‚úÖ
- Text: 5 texts, 2 responses (40%) ‚úÖ
- Trend: Outreach up 40%, reactions down 15%
```

**Analysis:** Mass emailing without engagement (spray and pray). Email quality poor. Phone/text work great - do more of those. **Action:** Cut email volume, increase quality and personalization.

### Part 3: Set Activity Goals

1. **Go to Agenda ‚Üí Goals** (if using Goals feature)
2. **Set weekly targets:** Outreach goal 50/week, reaction goal 15/week
3. **Track progress daily, adjust based on analytics**

**Using Goals + Analytics Together:**
- **Goals** = forward-looking targets
- **Analytics** = backward-looking results
- **Monthly:** Compare goals vs. actual, adjust next month

**WHAT IF (Text)**

**Q: "Outreach is high but reactions are low. What's wrong?"**
- A: Quality over quantity. Reaching out to too many cold contacts or messages aren't compelling. Focus on fewer, warmer contacts with personalized outreach.

**Q: "How improve email open rate?"**
- A: Better subject lines (personalized, specific), send at optimal times (9-11am or 1-3pm), clean list (remove non-responders), segment better.

**Q: "What's a 'good' outreach number per week?"**
- A: Depends on business. Ninja Selling suggests 25-50 meaningful outreaches/week. Track what converts for YOU, optimize from there.

**Q: "Does Cloze count automated campaign emails as outreach?"**
- A: Yes, if sent from your account. Can skew numbers if large drip campaigns running.

## MODULE AN3: PIPELINE ANALYTICS

### Understanding Your Deal Flow

**WHY (Video - 3-4 min)**

"How many deals are in your pipeline right now? How many are Hot vs. Warm? How long do they stay in each stage? Pipeline Analytics answers this instantly. No more guessing!"

**WHAT (Text + Visual)**

### Pipeline Metrics Explained

**Snapshot Metrics (Point in Time):**
- Total active buyers (Warm + Hot)
- Total active sellers (Warm + Hot)
- Total in contract
- Distribution across stages

**Flow Metrics (Over Time):**
- New leads added this month
- Leads that moved Warm ‚Üí Hot
- Leads that moved Hot ‚Üí In Contract
- Deals closed
- Deals lost

**Time Metrics:**
- Average time in each stage
- Time from first contact to close
- Bottlenecks (stages where deals stall)

**Example Pipeline Report:**
```
Current Pipeline (Jan 15, 2025):
Buyers: Warm 24, Hot 8, In Contract 3
Sellers: Warm 12, Hot 4, In Contract 2
Total Active Deals: 53

Movement This Month:
Buyers: New leads 15, Warm ‚Üí Hot 5, Hot ‚Üí Contract 2, Closed 1, Lost 3
Conversion Rates: Warm ‚Üí Hot 21% (5 of 24), Hot ‚Üí Contract 25% (2 of 8), Contract ‚Üí Closed TBD
```

**HOW (Video - 4-5 min)**

*Screen recording showing:*

### Part 1: View Pipeline Overview

1. **Go to Analytics ‚Üí Pipeline**
2. **View current snapshot:** Deals by Stage (chart/graph), total pipeline value (if using Properties with values)
3. **Select time period for trends:** This month, this quarter, YTD

### Part 2: Analyze Stage Distribution

**Healthy Pipeline Distribution:**
```
Warm: 60-70% (building relationships)
Hot: 20-30% (active negotiations)
In Contract: 5-10% (closing deals)
```

**Problem Distributions:**

**Too Many Warm, Few Hot:**
```
Warm: 95%, Hot: 4%, Contract: 1%
```
- **Problem:** Not moving people through pipeline
- **Fix:** More aggressive follow-up, better qualifying

**Too Many Hot, Nothing Closing:**
```
Warm: 20%, Hot: 70%, Contract: 10%
```
- **Problem:** Hot buyers not finding properties, or marking people Hot prematurely
- **Fix:** Better property matching, realistic Hot classification

### Part 3: Identify Bottlenecks

Deals stall at Hot stage for 90+ days

**Analysis:**
1. **Filter:** Stage = Hot + Added >90 days ago
2. **Export list, review individually:** What's blocking them? Unrealistic expectations? Waiting for external factor?
3. **Action:** Re-qualify or move to Lost

**WHAT IF (Text)**

**Q: "What's a 'good' pipeline size?"**
- A: 3x your monthly target in Warm/Hot combined. Want 2 closings/month? Maintain 6+ active Hot buyers.

**Q: "How long should someone stay in Warm?"**
- A: 30-90 days typically. Beyond that, move to Hot (progressing) or Lost (not).

**Q: "Pipeline is empty?"**
- A: Lead generation problem. Focus on: sphere outreach, open houses, online leads, referral requests. Can't work empty pipeline.

## MODULE AN4: CONVERSION ANALYTICS

### Where Leads Drop Off

**WHY (Video - 3-4 min)**

"100 leads come in. 10 close. What happened to the other 90? Conversion Analytics shows you exactly where they fall out. The result: Fix the leak. Double your closings without doubling your ad spend!"

**WHAT (Text + Visual)**

### The Conversion Funnel

**Typical Real Estate Funnel:**

```
Lead (SOI/New Contact)
    ‚Üì (Classify & Qualify)
Warm Buyer/Seller
    ‚Üì (Nurture & Educate)
Hot Buyer/Seller
    ‚Üì (Active Showings/Listing)
In Contract
    ‚Üì (Navigate Inspection/Financing)
Closed Deal
```

**Conversion Metrics to Track:**

| Stage Transition | Healthy Rate | Red Flag Rate | What It Measures |
|------------------|--------------|---------------|------------------|
| **Lead ‚Üí Warm** | 30-50% | <20% | Lead quality & qualification |
| **Warm ‚Üí Hot** | 15-25% | <10% | Nurturing effectiveness |
| **Hot ‚Üí Contract** | 30-50% | <20% | Showing/listing effectiveness |
| **Contract ‚Üí Closed** | 75-90% | <60% | Transaction management |

**Example Conversion Report:**
```
Q4 2024 Conversions (Buyers):
100 New Leads ‚Üí 42% ‚Üí 42 Warm ‚Üí 19% ‚Üí 8 Hot ‚Üí 50% ‚Üí 4 In Contract ‚Üí 75% ‚Üí 3 Closed Deals
Overall Lead ‚Üí Close: 3% ‚ö†Ô∏è
```

**Analysis:** Lead ‚Üí Warm good (42%), **Warm ‚Üí Hot weak (19%)** ‚¨ÖÔ∏è BOTTLENECK, Hot ‚Üí Contract solid (50%), Contract ‚Üí Close good (75%)

**Action:** Focus on Warm nurturing - improve follow-up, property matching, relationship building

### Calculating Your Conversion Rates

**Manual Method (if Cloze doesn't auto-calculate):**
1. **Export contacts by Stage**
2. **Count transitions over time period:** How many started as Warm on Jan 1? How many moved to Hot by Jan 31? Conversion rate = (Hot / Warm) √ó 100
3. **Track monthly, identify trends**

**HOW (Video - 5-6 min)**

*Screen recording showing:*

### Part 1: Access Conversion Analytics

1. **Go to Analytics ‚Üí Conversions** (if available)
2. **Select funnel:** Buyer, seller, or combined
3. **Select time period:** Last month, last quarter, or YTD
4. **View conversion rates:** Stage-to-stage percentages, visual funnel diagram, drop-off points highlighted

### Part 2: Identify Problem Areas

Low Warm ‚Üí Hot conversion

**Steps to Diagnose:**

1. **Filter:** Warm buyers added 90+ days ago, still Warm
2. **Export list, review patterns:** Following up regularly? (Check timeline) Responding? (Check last contact date) Know their criteria? (Check custom fields)
3. **Common issues:**
   - Infrequent follow-up (fix: set reminders)
   - Not sending listings (fix: proactive property sending)
   - Mis-classified as Warm when cold (fix: move to Lost)

### Part 3: Improve Conversion Rates

**Tactic 1: Shorten Time in Stage**
- **Track:** Average days in Warm stage
- **Goal:** Reduce from 90 days to 60 days
- **How:** More frequent touchpoints, better qualifying

**Tactic 2: Re-Qualify Regularly**
- **Every 30 days:** Review all Warm contacts - Still looking? Timeline changed? Ready to move to Hot?
- **Action:** Hot, Lost, or stay Warm with updated timeline

**Tactic 3: Automate Nurturing**
- **Use Campaigns:** Drip content to Warm buyers/sellers - keeps them engaged without manual outreach, moves them toward Hot with educational content

**WHAT IF (Text)**

**Q: "Lead ‚Üí Warm rate low (<20%)?"**
- A: Lead quality problem. Sources sending unqualified/uninterested people. Improve qualification questions or change lead sources.

**Q: "Hot ‚Üí Contract rate low (<20%)?"**
- A: Either Hot criteria too loose (marking people Hot prematurely), or property matching/showing skills need work.

**Q: "How know if conversion rates good?"**
- A: Compare to own historical data (improving or declining?), then benchmark industry averages. YOUR trend matters more than industry comparison.

## MODULE AN5: LEAD SOURCE & ROI ANALYTICS

### Know What Marketing Works

**WHY (Video - 3-4 min)**

"You spend $10K/month on marketing. Which $5K should you kill? Which $5K should you double? Lead source analytics tells you exactly where your money goes to die and where it multiplies!"

**WHAT (Text + Visual)**

### Tracking Lead Sources

**Required Setup:**
- **Custom Field:** "Lead Source" (Dropdown)
- **Options:** Zillow, Realtor.com, Facebook Ads, Google Ads, Referral - Past Client, Referral - Agent, Open House, SOI, Yard Sign, Walk-In, Other
- **Every new lead ‚Üí assign Lead Source immediately**

### ROI Calculation

**Formula:** ROI = (Gross Commission from Source - Cost of Source) / Cost of Source √ó 100%

**Example:**

**Zillow:** Monthly $3K, annual $36K, leads 120/year, closed 4, avg commission $9K, gross $36K ‚Üí **ROI: 0%** (break even)

**Facebook Ads:** Monthly $1.5K, annual $18K, leads 80/year, closed 8, avg commission $9K, gross $72K ‚Üí **ROI: 300%** üî•

**Action:** Kill Zillow (or reduce spend), double Facebook

### Lead Source Report Structure

| Lead Source | Leads | Warm | Hot | Closed | Conv % | Cost | Comm | ROI |
|-------------|-------|------|-----|--------|--------|------|------|-----|
| Zillow | 120 | 45 | 10 | 4 | 3.3% | $36K | $36K | 0% |
| Facebook | 80 | 35 | 12 | 8 | 10% | $18K | $72K | 300% |
| Referral - PC | 30 | 25 | 15 | 10 | 33% | $0 | $90K | ‚àû |
| Open House | 50 | 15 | 5 | 2 | 4% | $2K | $18K | 800% |
| SOI | 40 | 30 | 10 | 5 | 12.5% | $0 | $45K | ‚àû |

**Insights:** Best ROI = Past client referrals, SOI (free sources). Best paid ROI = Facebook, Open Houses. Worst ROI = Zillow (break-even). **Action:** Invest more in referral programs, open houses, Facebook

**HOW (Video - 5-6 min)**

*Screen recording showing:*

### Part 1: Set Up Lead Source Tracking

1. **Create custom field:** "Lead Source" (see Deep Dive 8)
2. **Add dropdown options** (all marketing channels)
3. **For every new contact:** Assign lead source immediately
4. **For closed deals:** Create custom field "Gross Commission"

### Part 2: Generate Lead Source Report

**Manual Method (Export & Analyze):**
1. **Export all contacts** with fields: Name, Lead Source, Segment/Stage, Closing Date, Gross Commission
2. **Open in Excel/Google Sheets**
3. **Create pivot table:** Rows = Lead Source, Values = Count of contacts + Sum of Commission
4. **Add costs manually** (from ad platforms), **Calculate ROI** per source

**Automated Method (if Cloze supports):**
1. **Go to Analytics ‚Üí Lead Source** (if available)
2. **View lead distribution by source, conversion rates by source**
3. **Export for ROI calculation**

### Part 3: Optimize Based on Data

**Monthly Review Process:**
1. **First Monday:** Run lead source report for previous month
2. **Calculate:** Cost per lead, cost per closing, ROI per source
3. **Identify:** Top performers (scale), break-even sources (test improvements or cut), money losers (kill immediately)
4. **Adjust spend:** Move budget from losers to winners
5. **Track trend:** Is performance improving?

**Example Optimization:**

**Current Spend:** Zillow $3K/month, Facebook $1.5K/month, Total $4.5K

**After 3 months data:** Zillow ROI 0%, Facebook ROI 300%

**New Spend:** Zillow $0 (cut), Facebook $4.5K (3x increase) - Expected result: 3x more closings from Facebook

## MODULE AN6: EMAIL & CAMPAIGN ANALYTICS

### Measuring Email Effectiveness

**WHY (Video - 2-3 min)**

"You send 100 emails. 30 people open them. 5 people click links. 1 person responds. Email analytics shows you how to get that to 60 opens, 20 clicks, 10 responses. That's 10x more conversations from the same work!"

**WHAT (Text + Visual)**

### Email Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Benchmark | Red Flag |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Open Rate** | % recipients who opened | 25-40% | <15% |
| **Click Rate** | % clicked a link | 10-20% | <5% |
| **Response Rate** | % who replied | 15-30% | <10% |
| **Unsubscribe Rate** | % opted out | <1% | >5% |

**Campaign Analytics:** For drip campaigns and automated sequences - enrollment rate (how many contacts enter campaign), completion rate (how many finish sequence), drop-off points (which email causes most unsubscribes), conversion rate (how many take desired action)

**Example Email Campaign Report:**
```
Campaign: "8x8 Buyer Nurture"
Enrolled 50, Completed 32 (64%), Dropped 18 (36%)

Email-by-Email:
Email 1: Open 85%, Click 30%, Drop 0%
Email 2: Open 70%, Click 25%, Drop 2%
Email 3: Open 60%, Click 20%, Drop 4% ‚ö†Ô∏è
Email 4: Open 45%, Click 15%, Drop 8% ‚ö†Ô∏è

Analysis: Email 3-4 causing drop-offs
Action: Revise Email 3 content, test improvements
```

**HOW (Video - 4-5 min)**

*Screen recording showing:*

### Part 1: View Email Analytics

1. **Go to Analytics ‚Üí Email** (if available)
2. **View aggregate metrics:** Overall open rate, overall click rate, trends over time
3. **Filter by:** Date range, campaign type, audience segment

### Part 2: Campaign Performance

1. **Go to Campaigns ‚Üí [Your Campaign]**
2. **Click Analytics/Report**
3. **View:** Enrollment count, current active contacts, completion rate, email-by-email performance
4. **Identify weak emails** (low opens/high drops), **test improvements**

### Part 3: A/B Testing (Manual)

**Test subject lines:**

**Variant A:** "New listings in Henderson" - Sent to 25, open rate 20%
**Variant B:** "Found 3 homes that match your criteria, Sarah" - Sent to 25, open rate 42% ‚úÖ

**Winner:** Personalized subject line
**Action:** Use personalization in all future emails

**WHAT IF (Text)**

**Q: "Open rates terrible (<15%). What's wrong?"**
- A: Poor subject lines (too generic, spammy), bad send timing (weekends, late night), or list has too many cold contacts.

**Q: "People open but don't click or respond. Why?"**
- A: Content doesn't compel action. Add clearer CTAs, make links more obvious, ask direct questions requiring responses.

**Q: "How reduce unsubscribes?"**
- A: Send less frequently, segment better (right message to right audience), make content valuable (not just "checking in"), give options (pause emails without unsubscribing).

## MODULE AN7: CUSTOM REPORTS & EXPORTS

### Build Your Own Analytics

**WHY (Video - 2-3 min)**

"Cloze's built-in analytics are great. But sometimes you need custom reports. Commissions by quarter. Referrals by source. Time-to-close by property type. Exports let you build anything. Your broker wants proof you're crushing it? Here's your ammunition!"

**WHAT (Text + Visual)**

### Custom Reports You Can Build

**Report 1: Commission by Lead Source (Annual)**

**Data Needed:** Lead Source (custom field), Gross Commission (custom field), Closing Date

**Steps:** Export all closed deals (Stage = Closed) for 2024. Include: Lead Source, Gross Commission. Pivot in Excel: Group by Lead Source, Sum Commission. Result: Total earnings per marketing channel

**Report 2: Time to Close by Stage**

**Data Needed:** Date Added to Warm, Date Moved to Hot, Date Moved to Contract, Closing Date

**Steps:** Export closed deals with date fields. Calculate in Excel: Warm to Hot days, Hot to Contract days, Contract to Close days. Average each stage duration. Result: Know typical sales cycle length

**Report 3: Referral Source Analysis**

**Data Needed:** Referral Source (custom field - who referred them), Segment, Closed deals

**Steps:** Export all contacts with Referral Source filled. Filter to closed deals. Count referrals per source. Result: Who sends you most business (VIP them!)

**Report 4: Pipeline Value Forecast**

**Data Needed:** All Hot buyers/sellers, Expected transaction value (custom field or Property value)

**Steps:** Export Hot stage contacts. Include estimated sale price or budget. Calculate: Sum of all Hot deal values √ó Expected close rate (30-50%). Result: Projected income for next 90 days

**HOW (Video - 4-5 min)**

*Screen recording showing:*

### Part 1: Export Data for Custom Reports

1. **Create Audience** for specific data set (Example: All closed deals in 2024)
2. **Export audience:** Include all relevant fields (custom fields, dates, segments)
3. **Download CSV, open in Excel or Google Sheets**

### Part 2: Build Pivot Table Report

**Example: Commission by Lead Source**

1. **In Excel: Insert ‚Üí Pivot Table**
2. **Rows:** Lead Source | **Values:** Sum of Gross Commission, Count of Contacts
3. **Result Table:**

| Lead Source | Closings | Total Commission |
|-------------|----------|------------------|
| Zillow | 4 | $36,000 |
| Facebook | 8 | $72,000 |
| Referral - PC | 10 | $90,000 |
| SOI | 5 | $45,000 |
| **TOTAL** | **27** | **$243,000** |

4. **Add chart** (bar graph) for visualization
5. **Save report** for monthly updates

### Part 3: Monthly Reporting Routine

**First of Every Month:**
1. **Run standard reports:** Pipeline snapshot, new leads by source, closings last month, conversion rates
2. **Export to template Excel file**
3. **Update charts/graphs automatically**
4. **Review for trends:** Pipeline growing or shrinking? Conversion improving or declining? Lead quality changing?
5. **Adjust strategy** based on findings

**WHAT IF (Text)**

**Q: "Not good at Excel. Can I still do custom reports?"**
- A: Use Google Sheets (built-in tutorials), or hire VA to set up template reports you update monthly.

**Q: "How often run reports?"**
- A: Pipeline/activity: Weekly. Conversions/ROI: Monthly. Annual review: Deep dive once per year.

**Q: "Cloze doesn't track something I need?"**
- A: Create custom field, fill in going forward, build custom reports from exports.
