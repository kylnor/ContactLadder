### Call Scripts vs Text Templates

| Feature | Call Scripts | Text Templates |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Purpose** | Structure phone conversations | Speed up text messaging |
| **Format** | Question/response flow | Pre-written messages |
| **Flexibility** | Use as guide, adapt live | Use as-is or customize |
| **Best For** | Complex conversations (consultations) | Quick responses, confirmations |

## SECTION 1: CREATING CALL SCRIPTS

**HOW to Build Effective Scripts (Video - 4-5 min)**

### Script Structure (3-Part Framework)

**1. OPENING (10-15 seconds)**
- Identify yourself
- State purpose
- Get permission to continue

**2. BODY (1-3 minutes)**
- Ask questions
- Provide value
- Address concerns

**3. CLOSING (15-30 seconds)**
- Summarize next steps
- Set expectations
- Thank them

### Example: 8x8 Check-In Script

```
OPENING:
"Hi [Name], it's [Your Name] with [Brokerage]. How are you?

I'm doing my monthly check-ins with my favorite people. Got 2 minutes?"

[Wait for yes]

BODY:
1. "How's everything going with you and [Spouse/Family]?"
   [Listen, respond authentically]

2. "How's the house treating you? Anything need fixing or updating?"
   [Listen for pain points]

3. "Real estate market has been [interesting/crazy/busy] lately.
    Do you know anyone thinking about buying or selling?"
   [Referral ask]

4. "I'm always here if you need contractor recommendations,
    market updates, or just want to grab coffee. Sound good?"
   [Reinforce availability]

CLOSING:
"Perfect! I'll check in again in a month or two. You have my number
 if anything comes up. Thanks for the time, [Name]!"

[End call warmly]
```

**Time:** 2-3 minutes total

### Example: Buyer Consultation Script

```
OPENING:
"Hi [Name], thanks for meeting with me. I want to understand exactly
 what you're looking for so I can help you find the perfect home.
 I've got about 10 questions - should take 15 minutes. Sound good?"

BODY - QUALIFYING QUESTIONS:
1. Budget: "What's your comfortable price range?"
2. Pre-approval: "Have you talked to a lender yet?"
3. Timeline: "When are you hoping to move in?"
4. Must-haves: "What are your non-negotiables?"
5. Nice-to-haves: "What would be nice but you could live without?"
6. Location: "Which areas are you considering?"
7. Home type: "Single-family, condo, townhome preference?"
8. Decision-makers: "Who's involved in the decision?"
9. Motivation: "What's driving the move?"
10. Concerns: "What worries you most about buying?"

BODY - VALUE PROPOSITION:
"Here's how I work with buyers:
- I'll send you listings that match your criteria
- We'll tour properties on your schedule
- I'll handle all negotiations and paperwork
- You don't pay me - the seller does
- My goal: Find you the right home, not just any home"

CLOSING:
"Let's get you set up on property alerts. I'll send you listings
 this week. What days work best for showings?"

[Schedule next steps]
```

**Time:** 15-20 minutes

### Where to Store Scripts in Cloze

**Option 1: Notes (Simple)**
1. Create contact "Scripts Library"
2. Add note for each script
3. Reference when needed

**Option 2: Templates (If Cloze Supports)**
1. Settings ‚Üí Templates ‚Üí Call Scripts
2. Save each script as template
3. Access during calls

**Option 3: External (Best for Complex Scripts)**
1. Save in Notes app, Evernote, or Google Docs
2. Access on phone during calls
3. Link in Cloze notes: "Call script: [link]"

## SECTION 3: BEST PRACTICES

### Do's and Don'ts

**CALL SCRIPTS:**

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Use as guide, not word-for-word
- Practice until natural
- Adapt to conversation flow
- Listen more than talk
- End with clear next steps

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Sound robotic
- Rush through questions
- Ignore their responses
- Pitch before building rapport
- Skip the closing/next steps

**TEXT TEMPLATES:**

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Personalize with names
- Keep under 160 characters (when possible)
- Use correct grammar/spelling
- Include clear call-to-action
- Respond quickly (templates speed this up)

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Send walls of text (break into multiple)
- Overuse emojis (1-2 max)
- Use templates for sensitive topics
- Copy-paste obvious templates
- Text after 9pm (unless urgent/expected)

### When to Customize vs Use Template

**Use Template AS-IS:**
- Appointment confirmations
- "Got it" acknowledgments
- Standard scheduling questions

**Customize Template:**
- Emotional situations (offer rejected, deal fell through)
- Complex questions
- Personal conversations
- First contact with new lead
