**WHAT (Text + Visual)**

**What Are Next Steps?**

Next Steps are **repeatable checklists** that follow your workflow and help move clients through each Stage. They track:
- What tasks have been completed
- What tasks are pending
- What needs to happen next
- When reminders should fire

**Next Steps vs. To-Do Lists:**

| Feature | Regular To-Do List | Next Steps in Cloze |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Repeatable** | Noâ€”manual every time | Yesâ€”templates for each Segment |
| **Auto-Advance** | Noâ€”you pick next task | Yes (Process style) or Manual (Task List style) |
| **Linked to Contacts** | Tasks are separate from people | Every Next Step is attached to a contact |
| **Reminders** | Manual | Automatic based on due dates |
| **Templates** | No | Yesâ€”customize per Segment |

**The Two Styles: Process vs. Task List**

| Style | How It Works | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Process** | Steps advance automatically in order. Mark Step 1 done â†’ Step 2 becomes active. Sequential, enforced order. | Linear workflows where steps must happen in sequence (8x8 program, listing presentation) |
| **Task List** | Mark steps done in any order. Manually pick which step is next. Flexible, non-linear. | Flexible workflows where order varies (buyer showings, ongoing client management) |
| **Mixed** | Some steps auto-advance, some are flexible | Most real-world workflows |

**Key Insight:**

> "Process style = 'Do this, then this, then this.' Task List style = 'Here's everything that needs to happen, do them in whatever order makes sense.' Most agents use Process for structured workflows (8x8, listing prep) and Task List for ongoing relationships (Hot Buyers, Advocates)."

**WHAT IF (Text)**

- Q: "What's the difference between Next Steps and the Keep in Touch reminders?"
  - A: Keep in Touch reminds you to stay in touch based on time since last contact. Next Steps are specific tasks in your workflow. Both appear in your Agenda.

- Q: "Can I have multiple Next Step sequences running for the same contact?"
  - A: Noâ€”each contact has ONE active Next Step sequence based on their Segment. If you change their Segment, you can switch to a different Next Step template.

- Q: "What if I need to skip a step?"
  - A: Mark it complete even if you didn't do it, or delete the step. Process style requires completing steps to advance, so skipping = marking done.

- Q: "Can I see all my active Next Steps across all contacts?"
  - A: Yesâ€”your Agenda shows all Next Steps due today or overdue. You can also filter in People to see everyone with active Next Steps.

**WHAT IF (Text)**

- Q: "What if I want to reschedule just one occurrence without changing the whole series?"
  - A: When the task appears, edit the due date for that instance only. It reschedules that occurrence but doesn't affect future repeats.

- Q: "What if I complete a recurring task early?"
  - A: Mark it complete whenever you do it. The next occurrence will still schedule based on the original frequency (not from when you completed it early).

- Q: "Can I have different recurring tasks for different contacts?"
  - A: Yesâ€”each contact can have their own recurring tasks. Example: Sarah gets quarterly calls, Mike gets monthly calls.

- Q: "What if I want a task to recur on specific days (e.g., 1st and 15th)?"
  - A: Create two separate recurring tasksâ€”one for the 1st, one for the 15th.

- Q: "Can I see a history of past completions for a recurring task?"
  - A: Yesâ€”in the contact's timeline or your activity history, you'll see all completed instances with dates.

- Q: "What if I want to temporarily pause a recurring task?"
  - A: Delete the current instance and the task won't reappear until the next scheduled date. Or edit the task and disable recurrence temporarily.

- Q: "Can I set up recurring tasks that assign to different team members?"
  - A: Yesâ€”create the task, set recurrence, and assign to a team member. They'll see it in their Agenda on the recurring schedule.

- Q: "What's the difference between recurring tasks and Keep in Touch reminders?"
  - A: Keep in Touch is relationship-based (reminds you to contact based on time since last interaction). Recurring tasks are action-based (specific tasks on a schedule). Both are useful!
